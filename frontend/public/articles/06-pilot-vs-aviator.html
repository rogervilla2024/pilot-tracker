Introduction

The crash game genre has exploded in popularity, and aviation-themed entries have become particularly beloved. Two prominent games in this space are Pilot by Gamzix and Aviator by Spribe. While both feature aircraft and similar core mechanics, they offer distinct experiences.

This comprehensive comparison will help you understand the differences between these two games, their respective strengths, and what each offers to players. Remember: regardless of which game you choose, both have a house edge that cannot be overcome.

Important Note

Neither Pilot nor Aviator offers a mathematical advantage to players. Both have similar RTPs and house edges. This comparison focuses on experience, features, and preferences - not on which is "better" for winning, as neither provides positive expected value.

RTP and House Edge Comparison

When it comes to the mathematical backbone of these games, Pilot and Aviator are remarkably similar:

Pilot

  • RTP: 97%
  • House Edge: 3%
  • Expected Loss: $3 per $100 wagered

Aviator

  • RTP: 97%
  • House Edge: 3%

The identical RTP means that from a purely mathematical perspective, neither game offers an advantage over the other. Your expected losses over time are the same regardless of which you play. The choice between them comes down to other factors: aesthetics, features, availability, and personal preference.

Variance Comparison

Both games are high-variance, with the potential for both rapid losses and significant wins. The exact variance depends on your cash-out strategy rather than the game itself. With similar multiplier distributions to maintain the same RTP, variance profiles are comparable.

Gameplay Mechanics

The core gameplay loop is essentially identical:

  1. Betting Phase: Place bets before the round starts
  2. Takeoff: Aircraft begins ascending, multiplier increases
  3. Cash Out Decision: Click to cash out at current multiplier
  4. Crash/Fly Away: Round ends, non-cashed bets lose

Aviator Gameplay Nuances

Frequently Asked Questions

While Pilot and Aviator share the crash game format and aviation theme, Pilot is an original creation by Gamzix with its own design, features, and identity. Both are legitimate games from respected developers.

Which game has better odds?

Both have 97% RTP, meaning identical odds. Neither offers better mathematical chances of winning.

Can I use the same strategies in both games?

Yes, the core mechanics are the same. Any strategy (and the limitations of strategies) applies equally to both games.

Is one game more "fair" than the other?

Both games use provably fair systems and are from licensed, regulated developers. Both are equally fair within the context of having a house edge.

Which has higher maximum multipliers?

Pilot has a stated maximum of 10,000x. Aviator's maximum can vary by implementation but is typically similar. Both can theoretically reach very high multipliers, though such outcomes are extremely rare.

Can I find both games at the same casino?

Some casinos offer both, but not all. It depends on which game providers the casino has partnered with.
